Numbers as Bits

Numbers are used as a form of bits too for the display of information. A group of eight bits makes a byte. In the binary number system, only two numbers are used: "0" means off, while "1" means on.

Compression of Pictures

With lossless data compression, there is always the ability to use fewer bits while ensuring that all the original data is still present. In lossy compression, bits are reduced greatly, but only some recessed forms of data are retained. Compression helps reduce the time to transmit information and saves space.

Data ETL

Managing data is not easy. Often, it may be disorganized, incomplete, or incorrect, which means you may have to consolidate data from multiple sources.

Using Data Related Programs

Programs are used to process data, enabling users to gain knowledge. Programs can also be used to clean and filter digital data.

Big Idea 4 Notes

The Internet

The internet is a global network connecting devices using standard protocols. Data is transmitted in packets that travel independently and may take different routes. TCP ensures reliable data transmission, while IP assigns addresses and routes data. DNS translates human-readable domain names into IP addresses.

Fault Tolerance

Fault tolerance allows systems to continue operating despite failures. The internet is designed to reroute data if a connection fails, and redundancy improves reliability.

Parallel and Distributed Computing

Parallel computing splits tasks among multiple processors for efficiency. Distributed computing involves multiple computers working together over a network to solve problems.

Big Idea 5 Notes

Beneficial and Harmful Effects

Computing has both positive and negative impacts. Benefits include improved communication, automation, and medical advances. Harms include privacy concerns, job loss, misinformation, and mental health effects.

Digital Divide

The digital divide is the gap between those with and without access to technology, caused by economic, geographic, or social factors. It affects education, job opportunities, and economic participation. Solutions include policies, nonprofit programs, and infrastructure development.

Computing Bias

Computing bias happens when algorithms favor certain groups unfairly. Examples include biased facial recognition and hiring algorithms. Solutions include diverse datasets, transparency, and ethical design.

Crowdsourcing

Crowdsourcing uses collective intelligence for problem-solving, such as Wikipedia and open-source software. It can improve efficiency but may spread misinformation if not monitored.

Legal and Ethical Concerns

Issues in computing include data privacy, intellectual property, and cybersecurity. Ethical concerns arise in AI, data collection, and software piracy. Ethical guidelines help ensure responsible technology use.

Safe Computing

Safe computing protects personal data and security. Best practices include strong passwords, two-factor authentication, and avoiding suspicious links.
